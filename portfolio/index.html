About me
I was born and raised in Washington DC. I attended UVA from 2007-2011 and obtained a B.S. in Systems & Information Engineering. After college I went to work for The Health Management Academy, an executive-focused health care consulting company based in Alexandria, VA. My career there began as a research analyst, working mostly on compiling and analyzing our company's survey data. Due to my growing interest in web development, I gradually began helping our IT Department with major projects, including the implementation of our company's first CRM system and delivering The Health Management Academy Meeting App. Not long after that I decided I wanted to dive full on into the world of IT, and accepted a promotion to be the IT Systems Manager.
Wanting to improve my technical prowess, I decided to attend General Assembly's part-time Back End Web Development course at their DC campus. It gave me a taste of what it is like to develop a web application from the ground up. Coming from a business world where all of the company's applications were SaaS that we purchased, I loved finally being able to have control over all aspects of an application, from the layouts and UI to the routing and database relationships.
This past spring, I decided to follow my passion for programming out to San Francisco to spend 3 months participating in General Assembly's Web Development Immersive. During this time, I have added a number of new skills to my toolbelt, but in particular fell in love with the MEAN stack and using Javascript on both the front and back end. With the recent arrival of ES6 and the rise in popularity of new MVC frameworks for Node like Sails JS, it makes me very excited to be a Javascript developer in this day and age. I'm excited to continue learning as I search for jobs that will test my skills and push me to become the best developer I can be.
